Why Omnipeak Exists

I started Omnipeak because I saw a gap in the market. Small businesses were either being ignored by the big consulting firms or getting sold overcomplicated solutions they didn't need. Meanwhile, the "cheap" options often left them with more problems than they started with.

Having spent years as a Developer Experience engineer optimizing CI/CD pipelines at a major flash memory company, I know what good technology looks like—and more importantly, I know how to make it work efficiently. The same principles that make enterprise software development smooth can transform how a small business operates.

The Omnipeak Difference

When you work with Omnipeak, you work directly with me. There's no account manager, no junior consultant learning on your dime, no communication games. You get my full attention and expertise on every project.

I believe in solutions that are right-sized for your business. Sometimes that means building something custom. Sometimes it means recommending a simple off-the-shelf tool. And sometimes it means telling you that what you already have is working fine. My job is to give you honest advice, not to maximize billable hours.

My Philosophy

Technology should make your life easier, not harder. It should save you time, reduce errors, and help you focus on what you do best—running your business. Every recommendation I make is filtered through that lens: will this actually help?

Practical Over Fancy

The best solution is one that actually gets used. I prioritize simplicity and usability.

Honest Advice

If something won't help you, I'll say so—even if it means less work for me.

Your Success First

I measure my success by your results, not by project size or complexity.

Long-term Thinking

Solutions that scale with your business, not quick fixes that create future problems.

Let's Work Together

Whether you're drowning in manual processes, confused about which software to use, or just know that something could be better—I'd love to help. The first conversation is always free, and there's never any obligation.
